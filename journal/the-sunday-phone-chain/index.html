The Sunday phone chain

Thomas K. · July 2026

My phone rang on a Sunday night with a simple question, and not one of us could answer it. That is the whole story, and it is exactly why I am building this.

The call came from a manager. He had just been called by one of his own guys, who wanted to know one thing: did a delivery of material make it out to the site the week before, and when. It is not a trick question. It is a yes or a no. And between the three of us, on a Sunday night, nobody actually had it.

So it turned into a chain. The guy on the site called the manager, the manager called me, and I stood in my kitchen trying to reconstruct a Thursday from memory. Did I run that load myself? Did it go on the truck, or is it still sitting at the yard under a tarp? Nobody had written it down, because when you are loading and driving and unloading all day, writing it down is the first thing that gets skipped.

Here is the part that got me. I spend my days building an app whose entire reason for existing is answering that exact question: where is the thing, and did it get there. And there I was on a Sunday night, doing it the oldest way there is. Memory, a guess, three phone calls, and a shrug. I had to laugh at myself a little. I am the guy living the problem while I build the fix for it.

In the app, that whole Sunday night is a three-second check. You open the job site's record and you see what was sent there, and when. No digging through texts. No trying to picture last Thursday. No waking somebody up to ask. The answer is just sitting there, because someone logged it in the few seconds it takes, on the phone that was already in their hand.

And it is not only the person in the field who gets that. The manager who called me could have pulled up the same record on a screen at his desk and seen it for himself, without calling anyone at all. That is the part I keep coming back to: the answer should not depend on catching the one person who happened to load the truck. It should just be there, for whoever needs it.

I want to be straight about the sting in this one. I could not run that check either, not yet. The field app is still working its way toward being in real hands, and until it is, I am stuck answering these questions the same way everyone else does. Building the cure and still catching the cold.

But that is exactly why I write these down. Every one of these Sunday nights is a reminder of why this has to exist, and of what good actually looks like when it does: a question that used to eat an evening, answered before you have finished asking it. That is the whole idea. More soon.

Thomas K.
